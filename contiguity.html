Neural Contiguity
(The Genius Curve)

Prologue

I started writing this awhile back, mainly focusing on the idea of scale-free networks being the core driver of dividends on knowledge - and what to do about it if it were true. I've now included a bit more than that, mainly with the what to do about it part. Neural-network-as-metaphor serves as a useful basis for speculating on what prescriptive ways to think might be. Needless to say, this comes up a lot for me.

If you're willing to bear a smidge of crackpottery, what is true in the following video covers much of the same conceptual ground that I would hope to. In particular, how fractal structures can come up through persistent accumulation and how this relates to neurons.

The concepts written below are pretty core to my thinking and thus my writing. I'll be referencing this post often.

Parode

"Knowledge is compound interest."[0]; "Knowledge is a percolation process."[1]; "Knowledge is a semantic web."[2]*: all three metaphors have (to my knowledge) been given independently; yet all reflect the same underlying model of there being exponential curve for returns on learning. A sign if there ever was one.

From Nature, "Anomalous critical and supercritical phenomena in explosive percolation"

We can look at our perceptions of genius or stupidity as compressions or stretches on this curve: a multiplier effect on knowledge accumulation, which is already a multiplier of itself.


From Better Explained, "An Intuitive Guide To Exponential Functions"

Exponential curves have their lopsided distribution of utility on the right. To see a genius is to see a person that appears to demonstrate knowledge and intelligence in a way which is more than exemplary, going beyond the class of its type. To say that knowledge "grows" is to involve an element of time; to define a genius is to say that someone is ahead of the curve, so to speak, in contrast to the typical population.

Between "saving up money", "increasing p_bond", and "connecting concepts", what can we infer about genius if all it is, is the long-run accumulation process of knowledge?
